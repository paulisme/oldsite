Music
Harmony Central - One of my very favorite web sites. Has tons of music resources for guitar, keyboard, etc. Also has a search engine for tablature (uses OLGA which was recently shut down, but there are still mirror sites out there). Check it out.
Web Riffs - A really cool site for guitarists to upload their own sound files. This site has a big archive of musician's interpretations of popular songs and also original compositions.
Synth Site - A collection of reviews made by synth users themselves. Includes all the popular keyboard makers (Alesis, Ensoniq, Korg, Kurzweil, Roland, etc.).
Korg Online - My favorite synth.
Music Boulevard - A good place to order CD's and cassettes on-line. RealAudio samples are available for most CD's so you know what you're getting before you get it.
RealNetworks - The free RealPlayer is in my opinion the most useful web plug-in there is. You really need it to get the full impact of the web and it's multimedia capabilities.
 
HTML and Web Design Resources
Microsoft Internet Explorer - My personal favorite of the big 2, probably because of its ActiveX capabilities.
Netscape Navigator - A good web designer always has both IE and Netscape. While your page may look great on one browser, it may look like junk on the other. This way you'll have a way to preview what it's going to look like.
Web Diner - This is where I got started when they had a section on AOL. A great site to learn the basics of HTML and links to other Web resources.
Builder.Com - A good web site for both the beginner and the advanced web designer. They've got some great tips.
XOOM - Probably the best web-hosting service out there. They provide 11 megs of server space and absolutely no pop-up ads and no irritating tilde (~) in the URL. What's the catch? XOOM is a marketing agency, and they e-mail you offers to buy their stuff all the time. I suggest getting a free Hotmail account and use that as your e-mail address for your XOOM account, that way all their junk mail won't go to your main e-mail address.
Tripod - A good web hosting site. They give you 10 megs of server space in exchange for a pop-up advertisement and the little annoying "~" in your URL (the "~" is probably for you to get the premium membership they offer).
GeoCities - Not the greatest web hosting service in the world, but certainly a popular one. The URLs are a bit too long. They also offer free e-mail accounts.
AngelFire - I'm not quite sure what to say about this web hosting service, since the last time I tried to build a web site with them, they only offered 35 KB of server space. I've heard good things about them lately, though, so it's probably worth checking out.
Hotmail - The first free web-based e-mail service, and probably the best (they've recently been bought out by Microsoft, so they must be doing something right). A great place to get an e-mail address if you don't have one already, or if you have one but need another.
LinkExchange - A must if you're looking for hits. This is a banner ad service that shows your banner on another web page around the net for every two times one of their's shows up on yours. They also track your web traffic, where you can check your stats to see how many hits you've had in the last hour, the last 24 hours, or the past month. An all-around excellent service, and the best part - it's free.
DreamBook - a good service if you want a guestbook on your web site.
WWWAdmin Home Page - A must visit if you use AOL as your web host. This page tells you how to make a guestbook, e-mail forms, and more. NOTE: this only works if your page is on AOL.
JASC, Inc. - This company makes a great software title called Paint Shop Pro. This shareware program is a a must-have for web graphics.
